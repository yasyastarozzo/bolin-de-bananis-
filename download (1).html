8 big pop songs you didn't know were written by Bruno Mars

Including a Sugababes hit.

By Lewis CornerPublished: 22 November 2016

Bruno Mars has delivered yet another collection of pop gold on his third studio album 24K Magic, cementing his position as one of the biggest male solo stars in music.

But while the American singer-songwriter naturally keeps the biggest and best songs for himself – from 'Uptown Funk' and '24K Magic' to 'Grenade' and 'Locked Out Of Heaven' – he has been known to give other artists a bona fide chart hit too. At the end of the day, sharing is caring.

So which massive pop hits out there has Bruno secretly sprinkled his 24K magic on? Here are just eight of the most famous and, in some cases, most surprising.

1. CEELO GREEN: 'F**K YOU'

View full post on Youtube

After scoring a global hit with 'Crazy' as part of Gnarls Barkley, CeeLo Green needed a massive hit to cement himself as a solo artist in his own right. He called upon the services of a then rising star, Bruno Mars, to help him craft another earworm anthem. 'F**k You' – which actually comes under the guises of no less than five different titles: 'Forget You', 'FU', 'F**k You (Heartbreaker)' and 'Thank You' – was a smash in 2010, and has since sold over 6 million copies in the US. Not bad for a song actually aimed at the music industry itself.

2. THE VAMPS: 'CAN WE DANCE'

View full post on Youtube

Back in 2013, pop scamps The Vamps were given a helping hand by Bruno Mars for their big debut single. Yep, Bruno may not have wanted to confirm it at first, but the teen romance charm (sort of) of 'Can We Dance' is largely his handy work. Once you know that very fact and listen to it back, it's so obvious that you wonder how you didn't notice it first time around.

3. JUSTIN BIEBER: 'LOVE ME'

View full post on Youtube

Long before Bruno helped out The Vamps, he played a part in steering a teenage Justin Bieber to eventual world domination. Biebs' 2009 single 'Love Me' was co-written by the Hawaiian star. Well, apart from the chorus of course, which is an interpolation of The Cardigans' classic '90s hit 'Lovefool'.

4. ADELE: 'ALL I ASK'

View full post on Youtube

When Adele wanted a big sweeping ballad for her third album 25, who better to ask than the 'When I Was Your Man' hit-maker himself, Bruno Mars? But just because by that point a superstar in his own right, it didn't mean Adele was going to take any shit in the studio. "She walks into the studio, she's got all this attitude, she's a diva, she's like, 'I don't want to do this, I don't like that'," he revealed. "And then as soon as we hit a couple chords that she liked, we started rolling, and that's where we got that song from."

5. SUGABABES: 'GET SEXY'

View full post on Youtube

Before Bruno Mars became one of the world's most successful male popstars, he came up through the ranks as part of songwriting trio The Smeezingtons. Yep, those Smeezingtons who happened to work on Sugababes' final album Sweet 7, and its lead single 'Get Sexy'. Again here, Bruno interpolated a classic song into the track's chorus, but this time it was Right Said Fred's 'I'm Too Sexy'. This track was also the Sugababes' final song before they released their last remaining original member Keisha Buchanan.

6. ADAM LAMBERT: 'NEVER CLOSE OUR EYES'

View full post on Youtube

If there's one male singer out there who can match the soulful twang and vocal range of Bruno Mars, it's Adam Lambert. That's what made 'Never Close Our Eyes' such a good match for the American Idol runner-up, giving him plenty of room on the chorus for his voice to soar. Again, this was a case of Bruno giving away a storming electro-pop banger to another artist, while he focussed on a more guitar-led vibe for his own record, which at the time was Unorthodox Jukebox.

7. ALEXANDRA BURKE: 'PERFECT'

View full post on Youtube

Back around the time Alexandra Burke was topping charts with her debut album Overcome, there were so many rumours that she was going to team up with Bruno Mars. While most assumed that meant a rousing duet, it actually resulted in Bruno giving this R&B-tinged ballad to the X Factor champ. And once you know it's co-written by him, you can hear that it would've sat on his debut album Doo-Wops & Hooligans rather nicely.

8. FLO RIDA ft. KESHA: 'RIGHT ROUND'

View full post on Youtube

If you hadn't guessed already, early on in his career Bruno Mars just couldn't resist interpolating a classic hit into the chorus of one of his songs. The most successful case of this, though, was putting Dead Or Alive's 'You Spin Me Round (Like A Record)' on Flo Rida's 'Right Round'. It resulted in the track shifting 636,000 downloads in a single week in the US, making it the biggest ever in American history. Well, until Adele's 'Hello' smashed that record with 1.1 million sold in seven days back in 2015, that was.
